About the Founder

Meet Mia Shrank.

I'm a junior at Watchung Hills Regional High School in Warren, New Jersey. I've been making art for as long as I can remember and over the past few years, I've had the chance to study it seriously, training in Barcelona, Spain and Florence, Italy.

I grew up half Korean and half white, with a South Korean immigrant mom and an American dad. For a long time, I didn't fully know where I fit. I felt too Korean for some rooms, and not Korean enough for others. That feeling of existing in between — of blending in without quite standing out, and standing out when you just want to blend in — is something a lot of kids like me know well.

Art has always been how I process that. It lets me put something complicated and emotional onto a canvas or a page and say: this is real, this is mine, and this is worth looking at.

Blend In Stand Out came out of that. I wanted to build something that didn't exist for me when I was younger: a real platform where mixed-race and multicultural students could share their experience through art and be genuinely celebrated for it. Not as a diversity checkbox. As artists.

I hope you'll submit, share, and help make this what it can be.

— Mia
